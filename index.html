Learning the Natural Language of the Spinal Cord @ .NeuroRestore

Spring - Autumn 2024

Enhanced and recovered MRI image accuracy using advanced deep learning techniques, specifically creating a 3D version of masked autoencoders. By generating synthetic bending artifacts and training models to remove them, we significantly improved spinal cord segmentation and diagnostic precision. Additionally, we explored the latent space to further demonstrate the project's usefulness and potential impact in the field of medical imaging.

Deep Learning Neuroimaging Data Analysis Research

AI Tutor for Scientific Courses @ EPFL

Spring 2024

Developed an AI tutor using the GPT-2 model to assist students with scientific courses. The tutor provides explanations, answers questions, and offers personalized support. We used fine-tuning techniques like Simple Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) on both generative and extractive question-answering (on multiple choice questions), as well as Direct Preference Optimization (DPO). This project highlighted the potential of NLP in making complex scientific topics more accessible and engaging for students.

Deep Learning Natural Language Processing Data Analysis

Decoding Brain Signals for Movement Recovery @ .NeuroRestore

Autumn 2023

Developed a deep learning model to decode arm movement intentions from ECoG recordings, aimed at aiding movement recovery for spinal cord injury patients. Using CNN-LSTM, EEGNet, and CEBRA-based models, we significantly improved movement classification. This demonstrated how deep learning enhances motor function interpretation, showcasing its potential in movement recovery.
